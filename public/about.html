About Me

I'm a mature student at the University of Aberdeen, studying Biological Sciences. I'm an aspiring bioscience researcher who has had a love for the life sciences for as long as I can remember. Having started out in healthcare, I was later drawn to the topic of infectious disease, then to microbiology, before finally finding my home in the field of evolutionary ecology and genomics, especially in regards to insects, algae and marine life. My past experience in healthcare provided me many opportunities to acquire skills to optimise studying and working in the biosciences - assisting in the operating theatre at a prominent private hospital in Aberdeen for example required speedy problem solving, being able to think quickly on my feet in the face of adversity, a firm respect for safety protocols, and an eagerness to assist my peers, learn and adapt.

My Experience

University of Aberdeen

Jun 2022 - Aug 2022

My project was an investigation into phylosymbiosis - the theory that the functional relationship between microbial communities and host phenotypes should result in co-evolutionary patterns of phylogenetic divergence in the host and compositional divergence in the microbiome. Corals, with their incredibly diverse microbial communities, made ideal models for phylosymbiosis.

The project involved:

  • Acquiring 16S microbiome Illumina sequencing data and coral host COI barcode data through the NCBI SRA and BOLD databases respectively.
  • Using R-based workflows, DADA2 and phyloseq, to identify differences in microbiome diversity and structure among hosts.
  • Using MEGA11 software to create a host phylogenetic tree using COI barcodes.
  • Testing for correspondence between microbiome divergence and host phylogeny.
